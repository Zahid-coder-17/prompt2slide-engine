1. Definition

The Pillars of Salah are the essential actions and statements of prayer that must be performed correctly for the prayer to be valid.

If any pillar is intentionally or unintentionally omitted, the salah becomes invalid and must be repeated.

2. Why Pillars of Salah Are Important

Salah is a structured act of worship with fixed components.

The pillars:

Define the minimum requirements of prayer

Distinguish valid salah from invalid salah

Ensure uniformity in worship

Without fulfilling the pillars, the act is not considered salah.

3. Core Idea

The core idea of the pillars of salah is:

“Certain actions and recitations are mandatory and cannot be skipped under any circumstance.”

Unlike recommended acts, pillars must be performed.

4. Total Number of Pillars

According to mainstream Sunni fiqh, the pillars of salah are 14.

These pillars apply to every obligatory and voluntary prayer, unless physical incapacity exists.

5. List of the Pillars of Salah
1. Standing (Qiyām)

Standing upright while performing obligatory prayers, if one is physically able.

2. Opening Takbīr (Takbīrat al-Ihrām)

Saying “Allāhu Akbar” to formally begin the prayer.

This takbīr makes all other actions outside prayer prohibited.

3. Recitation of Sūrah al-Fātiḥah

Reciting Sūrah al-Fātiḥah in every rak‘ah.

Without it, the rak‘ah is invalid.

4. Bowing (Rukū‘)

Bowing until the hands reach the knees with the back level.

5. Rising from Rukū‘

Standing upright after rukū‘.

6. Standing Upright (I‘tidāl)

Pausing briefly in an upright position after rukū‘.

7. Prostration (Sujūd)

Prostrating with seven body parts touching the ground:

Forehead (with nose)

Two hands

Two knees

Toes of both feet

8. Rising from Sujūd

Lifting the head and body from prostration.

9. Sitting Between the Two Prostrations

Sitting briefly between the two sujūd.

10. Tranquility (Ṭuma’nīnah)

Performing each pillar with calmness and stillness.

Rushing invalidates the pillar.

11. Final Sitting (Tashahhud Akhīr)

Sitting at the end of the prayer.

12. Reciting the Final Tashahhud

Recitation of the final testimony while sitting.

13. Sending Blessings upon the Prophet ﷺ

Reciting ṣalāh upon the Prophet ﷺ in the final sitting.

14. The Two Taslīms

Ending the prayer by saying “As-salāmu ‘alaykum wa raḥmatullāh”.

6. Pillars vs Obligatory Acts

Pillars: Cannot be compensated

Obligatory acts: Can be compensated by sujūd as-sahw

This distinction is critical in fiqh.

7. Missing a Pillar

If a pillar is missed:

Prayer becomes invalid

It must be repeated

Sujūd as-sahw is not sufficient

8. Pillars and Physical Ability

If someone cannot:

Stand → they may sit

Sit → they may lie down

The pillar remains obligatory within capability.

9. Pillars of Salah and Intention

Intention (niyyah):

Is required

Exists in the heart

Is not verbalized

It is a condition, not a pillar.

10. Pillars in Every Rak‘ah

Some pillars repeat in every rak‘ah:

Standing

Fātiḥah

Rukū‘

Sujūd

Others occur only at the end.

11. Educational Importance

Understanding the pillars:

Improves prayer quality

Prevents invalid salah

Helps in teaching and correction

Builds foundational Islamic knowledge

12. Common Mistakes Related to Pillars

Skipping Fātiḥah

Rushing rukū‘ and sujūd

Improper standing after rukū‘

Forgetting final tashahhud

These directly affect validity.

13. Use in Islamic Education Systems

Pillars of salah are taught in:

Madrasah curriculum

Fiqh classes

Salah correction workshops

Beginner Islamic courses

14. Summary

The pillars of salah are the essential components that make prayer valid. Each pillar must be performed correctly and with tranquility. Missing any pillar invalidates the prayer and requires repetition.
