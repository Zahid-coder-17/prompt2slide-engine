1. Definition

The Hadith of Intention is a prophetic narration that establishes that the value and validity of actions in Islam depend on a person’s intention (niyyah).

This hadith is considered one of the most important foundations of Islamic belief and practice.

2. Text of the Hadith

The Prophet ﷺ said:

“Actions are judged only by intentions, and every person will have only what they intended…”

This hadith is narrated from ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb (raḍiyallāhu ʿanhu).

3. Primary Source

The hadith is recorded in:

Sahih al-Bukhari

Sahih Muslim

It appears at the very beginning of Sahih al-Bukhari, highlighting its importance.

4. Core Message

The core message of the hadith is:

“An action has no spiritual value without correct intention.”

Two people may perform the same action, yet receive completely different rewards based on intention.

5. Meaning of Niyyah (Intention)

Niyyah refers to:

The purpose behind an action

The inner motivation of the heart

The reason for performing an act

Niyyah:

Resides in the heart

Is not spoken aloud

Is required before worship

6. Role of Intention in Worship

Intention is required for acts of worship such as:

Salah

Fasting

Zakah

Hajj

Wudu

Without intention, these acts are considered invalid or incomplete.

7. Intention and Sincerity (Ikhlāṣ)

The hadith links intention with sincerity.

Correct intention → accepted action

Wrong intention → rejected or unrewarded action

Actions done for showing off (riyā’) lose reward.

8. Worldly vs Religious Intentions

The Prophet ﷺ gave an example:

Migration for Allah and His Messenger → reward with Allah

Migration for worldly gain → reward only what was intended

This shows that intention defines the outcome, not the action itself.

9. Legal Importance in Fiqh

In Islamic jurisprudence:

Intention differentiates acts of worship from habits

Distinguishes obligatory from voluntary acts

Determines validity of actions

Many fiqh rulings are built upon this hadith.

10. Scholarly Status of the Hadith

Islamic scholars state:

This hadith is one-third of Islamic knowledge

It governs inward actions

It complements hadiths about outward actions

Imam ash-Shafi‘i emphasized its central role.

11. Intention and Accountability

According to the hadith:

Reward is based on intention

Accountability depends on purpose

Even permissible acts become worship with good intention

Examples:

Studying → worship if done for Allah

Teaching → worship if intention is sincere

12. Intention in Daily Life

Islam encourages transforming daily actions into worship by intention:

Eating for strength

Sleeping for rest to worship better

Working to support family lawfully

Intention elevates ordinary actions.

13. Common Misunderstandings

Intention does not justify sinful actions

Good intention does not make haram halal

Intention must align with correct action

Both correct intention and correct action are required.

14. Educational Importance

This hadith is taught:

At the start of hadith studies

In fiqh foundations

In ethics (akhlaq)

In spiritual purification (tazkiyah)

It shapes Muslim mindset and behavior.

15. Relationship to Salah

Salah is invalid without intention

Intention specifies which prayer is being performed

Intention must be present at the start of prayer

Intention is a condition, not a pillar.

16. Summary

The Hadith of Intention establishes that actions are judged by their intentions. It forms the foundation of Islamic worship, ethics, and accountability. Without sincere intention, actions carry no spiritual weight, regardless of their outward appearance.
